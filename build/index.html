What an exploration in building the 'perfect' guitar - for me

  • Got my first guitar, way too late in life, for my birthday in June of 2012
  • Have been kind of infatuated by them since
  • The magnetics and electronics are fascinating
  • Guitars are a perfect blend of form and function
  • Hard to imagine a more expressive instrument

Why for fun and relaxation

  • To learn - by screwing up
  • To explore - by trying new things
  • There are no luthier schools around here
  • So I've been ripping up perfectly good ones to see how they tick
  • I've screwed up a few guitars but I've learned a lot

Aethetics less is more

  • With almost every guitar I've ever seen - I wanted to remove something
    • Paint
    • Pickguard
    • Size
  • Seldom have I wanted to add something
  • Even with the most resplendent paint jobs, I wonder what the wood would look like

Quality what I've learned about 'quality'

  • There is no best guitar - only different guitars in different price ranges
  • 90% of guitars are about 90% the same
  • The top 5% guitars cost 10 to 20 times as much
  • Of that 5%, 90% is subjective with few objective differentiations
  • There has been very little innovation over the last 50 years, at least in terms of guitars with magnetic pickups

Inspiration what resonates with me

  • Parker - size/weight
  • DBZ - beautiful and plays great
  • Koz
    • There's lots to like about Koz guitars
      • Curves
      • Digital lutherie
      • CNC use
      • Curves
  • Stranberg
    • Headless guitars scream 'modern' to me
    • I like the look without the head
    • Having the tuners on the body makes more sense to me

My perfect guitar will borrow from all of these

The Future is headless

My next, next guitar will be headless - using a lot from these exercises
  • Bridge
    • Floyd rose speedloader (though not traditionally deemed useful for headless)
    • Hipshot
    • ABM
    • Stranberg

Don't like

  • Plastic
  • Multiple pickups
  • Heavy
  • Exagerated, impractical shapes

Like

  • Fine tuners on a fixed bridge
  • Thin
  • Beautiful wood
  • Sexy Curves
  • Ergonomics - guitars that take into consideration your body and playing style
